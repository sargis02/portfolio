Why Gaming Made Me Want to Study Computer Science

📅 Posted: January 2026

For as long as I can remember, I’ve been a gamer. But while my friends were focused on beating the next level, I found myself obsessed with a different question: how does this actually work?

I’d spend hours watching YouTube breakdowns of game engines, fascinated by the invisible systems bringing virtual worlds to life. That curiosity became impossible to ignore. It pushed me to stop just playing—and start creating. I began with Scratch, moved to Python, and now I’m diving into web development and Java.

Games like Minecraft and The Sims shaped how I think. They taught me systems thinking—the idea that every action has a logical reaction. Now when I debug code, I don’t guess. I trace problems back to their root cause, just like figuring out why a contraption in Minecraft isn’t working.

Even today, gaming is still part of my life. After long study sessions, I unwind with GTA V, Red Dead Redemption 2, or PUBG Mobile. I’m a sucker for rich lore and world-building. But now, instead of just exploring those worlds, I’m learning how to build my own.

Getting Into Photography: A Beginner's Journey

📅 Posted: January 2026

I picked up photography about six months ago after borrowing my flatmate's old camera. I had no idea what I was doing at first — the photos came out blurry and badly exposed. But watching YouTube tutorials (I basically learn everything from YouTube) I started understanding things like aperture, shutter speed, and ISO.

Turns out photography and coding have a lot in common — there's a lot of trial and error, learning from mistakes, and the satisfaction when you finally get it right is the same. My favourite thing to photograph right now is street photography in Brisbane. There's so much going on and every shot is different. I use a mobile camera IQOO Z9 Turbo and edit photos using free software. Hoping to get a proper camera at some point!

Why the Beach Will Always Be My Happy Place

📅 Posted: January 2026

I've always loved to travel, but nothing pulls at my heart quite like the sea. Growing up, a trip to the coast wasn't just a vacation—it was where I felt most at peace. And for me, no place captures that feeling better than Cox's Bazar in Bangladesh.

There's something unforgettable about standing on the shore of the world's longest natural sea beach. The waves stretch endlessly, the salt breeze hits your face, and for a moment, everything else fades away. I've walked that sandy shoreline countless times, watching fishing boats dot the horizon and the sunset set the sky on fire. It's the kind of place where time slows down.

What I love most is how the beach offers both adventure and stillness. One moment you're riding the waves; the next, you're sitting quietly, letting the sound of the water clear your mind.

Cox's Bazar taught me that travel isn't just about seeing new places—it's about finding the ones that feel like home. For me, that will always be by the sea.
